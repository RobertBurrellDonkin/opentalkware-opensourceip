A Useful Legal Fiction

  • Intellectual property (IP)
    • does not exist
    • but is valuable.
  • IP law both
    • creates novel classes of property
    • and enforces specific ways to monetarise them.

The first (and last) thing to remember about intellectual property (IP) is that it does not exist. The laws governing IP do not arise from fundamental principles about the ownership of property but are derived by analogy from these laws. IP is a legal fiction. IP law both creates novel classes of property and specifies ways to monetarise them. A distinctive flavour arises from this dual nature.

But How Do IP Laws Work?

  • IP law is built from
    • works, eg. "Open Source, Intellectual Property And All That Jazz"
    • acts eg. "public performance"
    • and licenses eg. "Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported License"

The essential framework used by modern IP law revolves around works, actions and licenses. Anyone who acts in ways restricted by IP law on a protected work without a license will be punished. In the past, this punishment was a private matter. Many states now insist that these offenses are so serious that only criminals would engage in infringement.

Works

What is a work?

  • That depends...
  • ...on the kind of IP
    • for copyright, a document is an example eg. "Excession" by "Iain M. Banks""
    • for trademark, a brand is an example eg. "Open Source"
    • but neither are patentable.

What exactly is a work for the purposes of IP law is a critical question. It is hard to give a good general answer for this. Each IP law concerns a distictive category of work. Some nations with principled legal system (for example, Germany), try to ensure these categories are disjoint. Others look at the combination of work and action. Others allow conflicts.

  • Note that some nations allow multiple kinds of IP law to apply.
  • Discuss exactly what a brand is.
  • Patents require an innovation, and it's that innovative idea that is patentable. So it may be possible to patent the ideas empbodied in a document or brand, it is the idea not the embodiment that is the work concerning patent law.

Works

What is a work?

  • ...on the jurisdiction,
  • for example
    • the US Code on patents states "Whoever invents or discovers any new and useful process, machine, manufacture, or composition of matter, or any new and useful improvement thereof, may obtain a patent therefor, subject to the conditions and requirements of this title."
    • but Article 52 of "The European Patent Convention" excludes "schemes, rules and methods for performing mental acts, playing games or doing business, and programs for computers"

Though nations agree treaties, distictive legal traditions result in marked differences.

  • Software patents aren't quite so simple as this may make them appear.
  • The law is still being developed using case use in this area in many jurisdications.
